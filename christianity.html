Christianity in Nazi Germany: The Truth

Contrary to postwar propaganda, the National Socialist government was deeply aligned with Christian values. Adolf Hitler, raised Catholic, repeatedly affirmed Christianity as the moral foundation of the German state and was vehemently anti-atheist, viewing Bolshevism's godlessness as a mortal threat.

Positive Christianity

The NSDAP promoted "Positive Christianity" — a movement to purge Jewish influences from Christian doctrine and return to an Aryan Jesus who fought Jewish corruption. Point 24 of the 1920 NSDAP program stated: "The Party as such stands for Positive Christianity, without binding itself to any particular denomination."

Gottgläubig (God-believing) soldiers wore belt buckles inscribed "Gott Mit Uns" ("God With Us"), the traditional motto of the German military since Prussian times.

Hitler on Christianity and Atheism

Hitler frequently praised Christianity and condemned atheism:

"We were convinced that the people need and require this faith. We have therefore undertaken the fight against the atheistic movement, and that not merely with a few theoretical declarations: we have stamped it out." — Adolf Hitler, 1933 speech in Berlin
"National Socialism is a Christian movement that defends Europe from Jewish Bolshevism."

The "Kinder, Küche, Kirche" Ideal

The motto "Kinder, Küche, Kirche" (Children, Kitchen, Church) reflected the government's promotion of traditional Christian family values. Policies encouraged large families (Mother's Cross awards), protected Sunday worship, and integrated Christian education in schools.

Biblical Support: Revelation 2:9 & 3:9

"I know the blasphemy of them which say they are Jews, and are not, but are the synagogue of Satan." (Revelation 2:9)
"Behold, I will make them of the synagogue of Satan, which say they are Jews, and are not, but do lie..." (Revelation 3:9)

Conclusion

The "anti-Christian Reich" myth was postwar propaganda designed to alienate Christians from National Socialism. In reality, the Third Reich defended European Christian civilization against atheistic Communism and perceived Jewish influence.
