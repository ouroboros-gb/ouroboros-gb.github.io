Timeline

Key events in the development and challenging of the mainstream Holocaust narrative.
1915–1938

Newspapers repeatedly claim "6 million" Jews suffering or in danger (e.g., 1915 pogroms, 1919 Ukraine, 1938 Kristallnacht) — the number appears symbolically long before WWII.

1942–1944

First Allied reports of "extermination camps" emerge. Early claims include soap/lampshades from human fat, electrified floors, and steam chambers — later abandoned.

1945–1946

Nuremberg trials: Confessions obtained under duress, no forensic evidence for gassing presented. Initial death estimates wildly inflated.

1960s–1970s

Holocaust narrative solidifies in education/media. Claims of human soap and lampshades quietly dropped by mainstream historians.

1978

Richard Harwood publishes "Did Six Million Really Die?" — early popular revisionist work.

1985–1988

Ernst Zündel trials in Canada: Leuchter Report presented, Red Cross documents entered, Raul Hilberg admits no scientific proof of extermination policy.

1990

Auschwitz death plaque revised from 4 million to 1.1 million. Soviet Union admits Katyn massacre was NKVD, not German.

1990s–2000s

Internet enables widespread revisionist research. Germar Rudolf, David Irving trials bring more attention. "Holocaust denial" laws spread across Europe.

Present

Revisionism grows despite censorship. Independent chemical analyses (Rudolf, etc.) confirm Leuchter's findings of no mass gassing capability.
