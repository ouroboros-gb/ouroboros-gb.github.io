Common Objections

Addressing frequent criticisms and misconceptions about revisionist arguments.
What about all the survivor testimonies?

Many testimonies contain inconsistencies or physically impossible claims (e.g., geysers of blood, colored smoke from crematoria). Post-war conditions encouraged exaggerated stories for compensation or political purposes. Coercion during trials (e.g., Nuremberg) also played a role. Reliable forensic evidence trumps anecdotal accounts.

Why are there laws against Holocaust denial if it's false?

Truth does not need laws to protect it — only weak narratives require criminalization. These laws prevent open debate and examination of evidence, suggesting the official story cannot withstand scrutiny.

What about the Nuremberg trials and confessions?

Many "confessions" were obtained under torture or threat (e.g., Rudolf Höss's affidavit). The trials were victors' justice — Allied crimes (Katyn, Dresden) were ignored or blamed on Germans. No forensic proof of systematic gassing was presented.

Hitler was anti-Christian — he planned to destroy the church.

Hitler criticized certain church leaders who opposed the regime, but publicly and privately supported Christianity as a bulwark against Bolshevism. "Positive Christianity" was promoted in the NSDAP program, and policies protected traditional Christian values.

Isn't claiming Jewish involvement in Bolshevism an antisemitic conspiracy?

Historical fact: Jewish individuals were heavily overrepresented in Bolshevik leadership (Trotsky, Yagoda, Kaganovich, etc.). Acknowledging this is no more "conspiracy" than noting Irish involvement in American politics. The atrocities happened regardless of ethnicity — but the pattern is undeniable.

Why focus on this when there are bigger issues today?

The "Holocaust" narrative is used to justify modern policies, silence dissent ("antisemitism"), and extract reparations. Understanding historical truth is essential to breaking cycles of manipulation and guilt-based politics.
